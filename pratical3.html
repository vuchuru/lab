Srinivasa Ramanujan Iyengar

1887 - 1920

Great Indian Mathematician who transformed Number Theory

Srinivasa Ramanujan (1887–1920) was a self-taught Indian mathematical genius whose groundbreaking work transformed number theory, infinite series, and mathematical analysis. His collaboration with G.H. Hardy at Cambridge University led to major advances in pure mathematics.

Srinivasa Ramanujan - Mathematical Genius

Interesting Facts

  • Ramanujan solved complex mathematical problems without formal higher education.
  • He filled three notebooks with thousands of original mathematical formulas.
  • Many of his formulas were proved correct years after his death.
  • The famous number 1729 is called the Hardy–Ramanujan number.
  • He became one of the youngest Fellows of the Royal Society in 1918.
  • India celebrates National Mathematics Day on December 22, his birthday.
  • A 2015 movie titled "The Man Who Knew Infinity" was based on his life.
